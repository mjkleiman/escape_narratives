## Section A — Definitions (for this Exception)

**A.1. "Licensed Material"** means, for purposes of this Exception, not only the verbatim Puppy Escape narrative text, the verbatim scoring rubrics, and any supplied figures or recordings, but also the assessment construct they embody: the scoring criteria, scoring rubric, item structure, semantic-category and narrative-event mapping, and the methodology by which responses are elicited and scored. A work that reproduces, paraphrases, restructures, translates, or otherwise reimplements this construct in a manner that preserves its assessment logic is a derivative of the Licensed Material, whether or not it copies the original wording.

**A.2. "Internal Use"** means use of the Licensed Material exclusively by an entity's own employees, and by bona fide Research Collaborators as defined below, for that entity's own patient care or research activities.

**A.3. "Research Collaborator"** means an entity or individual engaged with the user in a genuine, non-fee research collaboration — for example, a co-investigator, a research partner under a collaboration or data-use agreement, or a co-sponsor of a study. A party is **not** a Research Collaborator merely because it pays for, subscribes to, receives as a service, or is a customer or client of, any offering that uses the Licensed Material.

**A.4. "Commercial Offering"** means any product, service, platform, application, scoring system, or other offering — whether delivered as software, as a hosted or cloud service, as an assessment or scoring service, or in any other form — that is sold, licensed, syndicated, or commercially marketed to third parties and whose value derives from the Licensed Material (i.e., for which a third party pays, in whole or in part, for access to, or the functionality of, the Licensed Material itself). For the avoidance of doubt, charging third parties for access to or the functionality of the Licensed Material is a Commercial Offering even if the underlying software is never itself sold or licensed.

**A.5. "Professional Service Fee"** means a fee charged by a healthcare provider, clinic, hospital system, research organization, or contract research organization to its own patients, payers, participants, or research sponsors for clinical care or research services that the entity itself delivers, where the fee is consideration for that entity's professional service and not for access to, or distribution of, the Licensed Material itself.

## Section B — Permitted Uses

**B.1. Clinical Use.** Healthcare providers, clinics, and hospital systems may use, digitize, and integrate the Licensed Material for the direct clinical assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of their own individual patients.

**B.2. Fee-for-Service Clinical Practice.** The Clinical Use permission in B.1 includes the right of healthcare providers, clinics, and hospital systems to charge a Professional Service Fee to patients or payers for clinical assessment or care services that incorporate the Licensed Material, provided the fee is consideration for the provider's professional service and not for access to, or distribution of, the Licensed Material itself.

**B.3. Research Use.** Academic institutions, non-profit research organizations, contract research organizations (CROs), pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies, and other entities may use the Licensed Material for their own research purposes — including clinical trials, observational studies, and other investigations, regardless of funding source (including industry-sponsored research) — and may integrate it into their own internal research data collection systems, electronic data capture (EDC) platforms, and internally developed research applications, provided such use is Internal Use as defined above. Such entities may charge a Professional Service Fee to their own research sponsors for research services they deliver that incorporate the Licensed Material, subject to the limitation in B.4 and the prohibitions in Section C.

**B.4. Limit of the Fee-for-Service Permissions.** The permissions in B.2 and B.3 authorize fees charged by an entity for **its own** delivery of care or research services to its own patients, payers, participants, or sponsors. They do **not** authorize offering the Licensed Material — or a product, platform, or scoring service whose value derives from the Licensed Material — to third parties for their use, which remains a Commercial Offering governed by Section C.

**B.5. Permitted Digital Integration.** Healthcare and research entities may integrate, automate, or digitize the Licensed Material into their own internal Electronic Health Records (EHRs), scoring systems, EDC systems, or internally developed clinical or research applications, provided such systems are used solely for that entity's own Internal Use and are not offered to third parties as, or as part of, a Commercial Offering.

## Section C — Prohibitions and Reserved Rights

**C.1. No commercialization of the Material.** This Exception does not permit commercialization of the Licensed Material. It is prohibited to incorporate, integrate, bundle, or otherwise use the Licensed Material in or in connection with any Commercial Offering. This prohibition applies regardless of whether the Licensed Material is used in its original or in derivative form, and regardless of whether the user characterizes its activity as clinical, research, or internal. Any entity wishing to use the Licensed Material in a Commercial Offering must first obtain a separate written commercial license from the Licensor.

**C.2. Relationship to the fee-for-service permissions.** For the avoidance of doubt, a healthcare provider's or research entity's charging of a Professional Service Fee for clinical care or research services delivered to its own patients, payers, participants, or sponsors under Sections B.2 and B.3 is **not** a prohibited commercial sale under C.1, even where such services incorporate the Licensed Material. The prohibition in C.1 applies to offering the Licensed Material itself — or a product, platform, or scoring service whose value derives from the Licensed Material — to third parties for a fee.

**C.3. No fee-based redistribution of the Material.** Except as permitted under the Professional Service Fee provisions, it is prohibited to charge any third party for access to, distribution of, or the functionality of the Licensed Material, whether or not any software embodying the Licensed Material is itself sold, licensed, or transferred.

**C.4. The prohibition follows the Material.** The permissions granted here attach to qualifying Internal Use and fee-for-service practice and do not survive a change in the nature of that use. If a system, tool, or dataset incorporating the Licensed Material is subsequently sold, transferred, spun out, made part of an acquired business, or otherwise made part of a Commercial Offering, a separate written commercial license from the Licensor is required at that time, regardless of how the system was originally built.

**C.5. Derivatives do not escape these terms.** Producing Adapted Material or any derivative of the Licensed Material (as defined in Section A.1) does not remove that material from the scope of this Exception or the Base License. A derivative that preserves the assessment construct remains subject to the NonCommercial restriction and to this Exception.
